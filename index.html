Projects



Aerial Filmmaking

As a high school student, I was fascinated by drones and aerial cinematography. The combination of technology and art made perfect sense and seemed like a never ending realm of possibility.
I began building my own drones and got licensed by the FAA to fly commercially (Part 107 license). To spread my name around the community I started working with local realtors to make short highlight reels of different properties. Eventually I was working enough that I was able to register my own aerial photography company and become the director of aerial photography for the media agency Creative Media Alliance (CMA).

Large Scale Fixed Wing Drones

While working as a UAV engineer with a collaborative team at Applewhite Aero, a Seattle based Drone manufacturing and design company, I was able to expand and learn a variety of valuable engineering skills.
I learned the intricacies of fiberglassing, laying carbon fiber, working with CNC mills, 3D printers, and large scale autopilot systems. However, most importantly I learned how to adapt to new problems that would arise while still working with a team. Keeping a level head while maintaining solid communication and working with a team was critical for success.

Surfboards

This project started because I felt that consumer surfboards were far too espensive, so I gave it a shot myself.
After much trial and error, my most recent design (pictured below) is optimized to be lightweight, yet still strong and flexible enough for a wide range of situations.
The concepts behind building sufboards turned out to be far more complex than I originally expected. Any slight change in the shape of the board can completely alter the way the board works. It was increadibly interesting to research and see how different physical alterations related to the underlying physics concepts. I learned more than I ever thought during this fun, personal project, that ended up saving me a lot of money.
During the construction phase of this project I learned how difficult working with fiberglass and epoxy can be. However, after enough tries, it is a medium I now feel extremely comfortable working with.
As with any project there is always room for improvement and I hope to continue this hobby into the future with a more solidified understanding of the physics that make these boards work.




3D Printing

3D printing has been a part of so many of my projects, whether it's for work or a personal project. At a certain point I decided to build my own printer. I settled on a Core XY design, optimized for speed and accuracy with a lightweight print head. After finicking with the arduino and troubleshooting a number of problems at the begining, I had a working printer. From that point forward almost all the projects I've worked on have used this machine for prototyping as well as final production. As a result of this project I have a deep understanding of the principles and software that control most addative 3D printing designs.
